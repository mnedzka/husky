5 steps

Define

Diverge

Decide

Prototype

Validate

Before design sprint participants can build anything, they need to deeply understand the problem.

Participants during Lightning Talks learn about:
Design principles – How is the form factor different from others?
Design challenge – What problem are they trying to solve?
Personas – Who are they designing for?

Once the participants are aware of the product, the design challenge, and their user, it's time to search for solution.

The diverge phase is the sensational time when anything is possible. Attendees work individually and collaboratively on diverse results with no borders in their creativity.

This is when critical thinking reentersthe picture. Coming up with with a bunch of great ideas is only useful if you can take the next step to build them.

Participants, all together, silently vote for most promising and efective solution. Their choicehas to go along with initials criterias and must be possible to build.

Now it's time for participants to transform their ideas into real prototype: website, interactive mock-up, promotion video etc.

Attendees use 'rapid prototyping' method to learn more about their ideas and get feedback from one another. It allows to test their ideas without nvesting a ton of time, money and resources.

This phase is the most important step. It allows designers and engineers to test their prototypewith users to learn what does and doesn't work.

Participants, based on previous goals and success metrics, prepare a user testing scenario.While one of the team is conducting the test, the rest is listening and making notes. Test's conclusions with all the stakeholders, is the final round of the workshop.
